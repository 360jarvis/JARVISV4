# Quality Command Center Blueprint

## Purpose

The Quality Command Center manages service quality after bookings are scheduled, completed, reviewed, inspected, or disputed.

## Core Rule

Quality is not separate from operations. Every quality event must connect to the booking, customer, workforce, payments, timeline, and reports.

## Required Capabilities

- Supervisor inspection queue
- Customer complaints
- Re-clean requests
- Damage claims
- Review tracking
- Photos and evidence
- Resolution workflow
- Customer timeline updates
- Workforce coaching visibility
- Financial credit or refund connection
- AI quality insights

## Quality Workflows

### Inspection

1. Select completed booking.
2. Assign supervisor.
3. Complete checklist.
4. Upload photos.
5. Mark pass or fail.
6. Create follow-up if needed.
7. Write timeline and audit events.

### Complaint

1. Customer submits issue or office creates issue.
2. Link complaint to customer and booking.
3. Categorize issue.
4. Assign owner.
5. Review evidence.
6. Decide resolution.
7. Schedule re-clean, issue credit, refund, or close.
8. Write timeline and audit events.

### Damage Claim

1. Record claim.
2. Attach photos and notes.
3. Link booking, customer, and assigned team.
4. Review liability.
5. Track resolution.
6. Connect financial action if needed.

## Quality Statuses

- New
- In Review
- Assigned
- Waiting Customer
- Re-clean Scheduled
- Resolved
- Closed
- Escalated

## Connected Modules

- Customer 360
- Booking Command Center
- Dispatch
- Workforce
- Payments and Financial
- Reports
- Notifications
- Jarvixx AI

## Beta Acceptance Criteria

- Quality records link to customer and booking.
- Re-clean workflow creates a connected booking action.
- Complaint timeline appears in Customer 360.
- Financial impact can be recorded.
- Workforce visibility is role protected.
- AI can summarize quality risk by customer, team, and service type.
