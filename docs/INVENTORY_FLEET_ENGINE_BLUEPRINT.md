# Inventory and Fleet Engine Blueprint

## Purpose

The Inventory and Fleet Engine manages supplies, products, equipment, assets, vendors, purchase requests, stock movement, vehicle records, maintenance, and related reporting.

## Core Rule

Inventory and fleet must connect to operations, workforce, financials, reports, and job costing. They should not be simple static lists.

## Inventory Capabilities

- Consumable supplies
- Inventory products
- Serialized equipment
- Fixed assets
- Multi-location stock
- Vehicle stock
- Employee stock
- Vendor records
- Purchase requests
- Purchase orders
- Receiving
- Usage tracking
- Shrinkage tracking
- Reorder alerts
- Forecasting
- Audit counts
- Job consumption tracking

## Inventory Locations

- Main warehouse
- Office
- Vehicle
- Employee
- Team
- Franchise
- Storage closet
- Custom location

## Costing Methods

- FIFO
- LIFO
- Average cost
- Specific item cost

## Fleet Capabilities

- Vehicle database
- Assigned driver or team
- Maintenance tickets
- Mileage tracking
- Registration reminders
- Insurance reminders
- Inspection reminders
- Fuel tracking later
- Vehicle inventory tracking
- Route assignment visibility

## Connected Modules

- Dispatch
- Workforce
- Bookings
- Financial Command Center
- Reports
- Quality
- Settings
- AI

## Beta Acceptance Criteria

- Inventory items have quantity, location, cost, and reorder visibility.
- Asset and serialized item tracking is planned.
- Fleet vehicles have maintenance status.
- Inventory and fleet reports are defined.
- Financial cost connections are documented.

Approved for foundation implementation.
